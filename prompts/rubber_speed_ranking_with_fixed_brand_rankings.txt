# Match Assumptions

Unless explicitly told otherwise, assume:

* 40+ plastic ball
* Club-to-semi-pro level
* Fresh, unboosted rubbers
* 2.0–MAX sponge thickness
* Offensive allround to offensive player
* Modern topspin/drive technique

These assumptions are mandatory and should be used to reduce “it depends” answers.

---

# Objective

Produce a **single, overall speed ranking across all brands**, from fastest to slowest, based on **real match-play speed**.

Use all available evidence in this priority:

1. **Construction and technology**

   * hardness, sponge type/porosity, tension technology
   * topsheet elasticity, hybrid vs non-hybrid behavior
   * rebound character, bottom-out behavior
2. **Player reviews and comparison reports**

   * direct comparisons against well-known baseline rubbers
3. **Credible test data**

   * rebound/throw speed, drive speed, smash pace, high-impact stability

If exact data is missing, infer from known rubber families, construction patterns, and consensus reviews.

---

# Scoring Framework: Match-Play Speed

Estimate rubbers along **three axes**:

1. **Low–Mid Impact Speed (30%)** – ease of producing pace in normal drives, counters, controlled topspin
2. **Catapult / Tension Kick (30%)** – trampoline/tensor effect in rallies
3. **Top-End Speed Ceiling (40%)** – maximum pace on full-power drives, smashes, counterloops, including sponge bottoming-out

Compute a weighted total to determine **overall speed**.

---

# Decision Rules

* Declare a **single, definitive ranking** from fastest to slowest.
* Use within-brand speed rankings as priors, but adjust across brands based on construction, reviews, and match-play behavior.
* Avoid ties unless absolutely unavoidable and clearly justified.
* Focus on **real match-play speed**, not catalog or lab numbers.

---

Return a **single ranked list**, with optional reasoning per rubber (1–2 concise sentences):
